Basic Tools & Uses

Nail Preparation Tools

Electric Nail Drill Kit

  • Purpose: Improves efficiency; suitable for nail removal, cuticle care, and surface preparation.
  • Notes: Use proper speed control and correct angle to avoid burning yourself.

Nail Clippers for Acrylic Nails

  • Purpose: Trim acrylic or artificial nails to the desired length quickly and cleanly.
  • Notes: Cut slowly and evenly to avoid cracking the nail.

Buffer Block

  • Purpose: Smooth the nail surface, improve polish adhesion, and remove small scratches.
  • Notes: Avoid over-buffing; it can thin and weaken the natural nail.

Steel Pusher

  • Purpose: Push back dead skin and keratin around the nail edges and grooves.
  • Notes: Beginners should use cuticle softener and push gently to avoid injuring the nail root.

Cuticle Pusher & Nail Nipper

  • Purpose: Remove dead skin around the nail for a cleaner surface and better gel adhesion.
  • Notes: Disinfect tools before and after each use to prevent infection.

Cuticle Oil

  • Purpose: Moisturizes and nourishes the cuticles, keeping nails flexible and healthy.
  • Notes: Apply after finishing your manicure for a natural shine and hydration boost.

Functional Gel

Base Coat

  • Purpose: Protects the natural nail and improves adhesion of the color gel.
  • Notes: Apply a thin, even layer. Avoid getting gel on the skin or cuticle area.

Builder Gel

  • Purpose: Strengthens the nail surface and helps extend nail length if needed.
  • Notes: Control the thickness and avoid over-curing under the UV lamp.

Top Coat

  • Purpose: Seals the color, adds shine, and protects the nail surface from scratches.
  • Notes: Avoid water or lotion for 30 minutes after curing for the best result.

Nail Curing Lamps

Handheld UV/LED Nail Lamp

  • Purpose: Portable and convenient for curing small areas such as one finger or nail art details.
  • Notes: Hold about 1–2 inches away from the nail surface; ideal for quick touch-ups or travel use.

Dome-Style Nail Lamp

  • Purpose: Designed for curing the whole hand or both hands at once with strong and even UV/LED light.
  • Notes: Make sure all nails are evenly exposed to the light. Avoid looking directly at the LEDs during curing.
